Air Travel Essentials

Before you head to the airport, make sure you have your boarding pass, valid ID, and any travel documents. Most airlines recommend arriving at least 2 hours early for domestic flights and 3 hours for international ones. Consider bringing a carry-on suitcase for convenience and speed through baggage claim.

Figure 2: Airport terminal check-in and security lanes.

Inside the Airport

Most airports are equipped with automated kiosks for check-in, along with self-service bag drops. Security checks are mandatory and may include scanning your items and walking through metal detectors. Remember to remove laptops and liquids from your bag for inspection.

Figure 3: Carry-on and checked luggage sizes vary by airline.

Packing Smart

Choose luggage that is durable and easy to maneuver. Essential items like chargers, headphones, and a travel pillow can make your journey smoother. Always check the airline's baggage policies to avoid surprise fees at the gate.
